Product Management Prep

Hello there, welcome! Landing that ever so elusive product manager role can be challenging but achievable with the right preparation!

My name is Ronak and I'm a product manager at PayPal, creator of one of the largest graphing calculator resources online, and UCLA graduate.

I’m with you on knowing what it feels like to apply for PM roles with limited experience. Throughout college, I applied to ~500 internship and full-time roles (I wish that number didn’t have to be so high lol). Anytime I came across a company that used Taleo, I mentally prepared myself knowing what should take 30 seconds would now take 15 minutes. Most of my applications ended with a rejection, but that’s okay. It only took one acceptance for me to break into product management.

Persistence with adaptability is key. If I knew what I know now back then, I would have been able to work smarter and better understand how to land PM roles.

As a product mentor and coach, I’ll provide straightforward and actionable guidance on how we can achieve your product management aspirations.




What I Can Help With


1. Breaking into Product Management

  • - High School, Undergraduate, or Graduate Student
  • - Working professional (Analyst, Designer, Engineer, etc.) looking for a career switch
  • - I’ll cover the pros and cons of what it’s like to be a product manager, the soft and hard skills needed to be successful, and together we’ll create an actionable plan for you to break into product management.

2. Resume Review and How to Land an Interview

  • - Having a good resume and company outreach tactics will be essential to landing an interview. This is the first hurdle to tackle on the road to becoming a PM.
  • - I will send over my resume as a reference and we will go over updating your resume structure and content to ensure that your resume stands out when recruiters and hiring managers are reviewing it.
  • - I’ll also go over some strategies you can use when you don’t hear back from a company such as figuring out a recruiter/hiring managers email and sending them a cold email.

3. Interview Prep

  • - Once your resume has been reviewed and the company wants to speak with you, we can work on how to be best prepared for different interview rounds (product strategy, improve a product, behaviorals, technical etc.) and then have a mock interview.

4. Product Management Guide (Free)

  • - Over the last 3 years, I’ve put together a detailed 50+ page PM Guide on topics ranging from product development lifecycle to how to perform a root cause analysis when a specific metric is going down.
